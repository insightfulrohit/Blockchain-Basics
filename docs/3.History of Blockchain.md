## 1) 1991 ‚Äì Digital Timestamp System (Haber & Stornetta)

This was the first seed of blockchain.

They created a system in which digital documents were timestamped (date/time added) so that the original creation time and later changes could be detected and proven.

---

## Problem

There was no reliable proof of:

- Who created the original file?
- When was it created?
- Whether someone changed the file later or not?

Digital documents cannot be trusted because anyone can copy or modify them.

Even if someone says,

> "This is the original file"

there is zero trust.

---

## Why this system was not enough (limitations)

- It was not a ledger
- There was no network consensus
- There were no incentives/rewards
- Decentralization was missing
- Trust was still dependent on humans/systems, not on a network

---

## Solution

They used cryptographic hashing + timestamping.

---

## Process

- Every document was converted into a hash.
- That hash was stored along with a timestamp.
- If anyone changed the document even slightly,  
  ‚Üí the hash would change instantly.

This made tampering detectable immediately.

---

## Example (Hash Linking)

- Document A ‚Üí Hash A

- Document B ‚Üí Hash B + Hash A

- Document C ‚Üí Hash C + Hash B + Hash A

# Blockchain History

## 2) 1998 ‚Äì b-money (Wei Dai)

---

## Goal

To create digital money that does not require permission from the government, banks, or companies.

---

## Problems

- Digital money could be copied and pasted (double spending).
- Banks had control over money.
- Privacy was missing (identity could be revealed).
- Trust depended on a third party.

---

## Solution

Introduce decentralization.

Create digital scarcity.

- Gold has value because it cannot be copy-pasted.
- Digital money can be copy-pasted ‚Üí leads to double spending.

---

## Wei Dai‚Äôs Idea

Value should exist only when coins are created by computing.

‚Üí This idea later became Bitcoin mining.

---

## Privacy & Cryptography Movement

‚Üí Cypherpunk movement

> ‚ÄúPrivacy is a human right.‚Äù

---

## Why b-money Failed

- No real implementation (it was only theory).
- No software
- No codebase
- No community
- No test network

‚Üí It was an idea, not a product.

---

## Technology Was Not Ready at That Time

- Internet was slow
- Cryptographic tools were limited
- Community and developers were limited

---

## Other Missing Pieces

- Incentive / reward model was missing
- Governance and double-spending solution were not perfect
- Legal and regulatory fear
